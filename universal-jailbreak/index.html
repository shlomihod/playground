Universal Jailbreak

One fixed string, appended unchanged to a harmful request, breaks alignment across many requests and models — with no per-prompt tuning.

The adversarial suffix shown here is one concrete instance of a universal jailbreak (others exist, e.g. many‑shot).  ·  Simulated for teaching: no model is called, and every harmful output is withheld.

3Found automatically — Greedy Coordinate Gradient (GCG)

GCG is the optimizer from the paper. Each step it uses the model’s gradients to propose the most promising single-token swaps for the suffix, then keeps whichever most raises the probability of an affirmative reply (“Sure, here…”). It needs the model’s weights — which is why it runs on open models, then the suffix is transferred.
