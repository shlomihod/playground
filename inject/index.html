🦊 The Red Fox — A Few Facts

A tiny field guide to the world's most widespread wild canine.

The red fox (Vulpes vulpes) ranges across more of the planet than almost any other wild carnivore — from forests and tundra to farmland and the middle of big cities. It is clever, adaptable, and famously hard to outwit.

Built for the pounce

A hunting fox listens for prey under snow or grass, then leaps high and dives nose-first in a move called "mousing." Studies suggest foxes may even line up these pounces using Earth's magnetic field — a living compass for judging distance before they jump.

A few things worth knowing

  • A group of foxes is called a "skulk" or a "leash."
  • Foxes make more than forty different sounds, including an eerie, almost human-sounding scream at night.
  • That big tail — the "brush" — helps with balance mid-leap and wraps around the fox like a scarf to keep warm in winter.
